Secret Ingredient: Chorus

How did we get here?

I decided at some point in December to run a recording competition on ilovefuzz.com. For some reason, I mashed up the idea of Iron Chef and got Iron Fuzz. The rules are simple:

  • Record a cover song.
  • Use the "secret ingredient" effect in your song.

My idea that was under constraints, people might be a little more comfortable recording and sharing their creation. (The constraints and light-hearted nature of the competition are there to help, and to prevent the kind of crippling perfectionism that plagues a lot of my own recording efforts.)

The thread where the competition was discussed follows the format of the Iron Fuzz show -- I've spared you from that style here. (But maybe in the future I will make the website more like a script for the show.)

Judging

I asked a select few judges to go over the submissions and give some feedback, as if they were consuming the Fuzz Chefs' culinary creations. Here are their responses.

(bronzetalon is an honorary judge for this round, because he also competed.)

Judge Kelly

Gearmond: Sounds like something went wrong with his TV. Where's the chorus?

blahquaker: I can't taste any of the War Pigs in this. You have overprocessed it too far from the original flavor.

Skullservant: So far we're getting a lot of minimal, noisy contributions, and this is one of those. It's hard to distinguish where the chorus is in there.

zRobertz: A little wobbly. An inventive effort, yet not satisfying.

bronzetalon: Creative use of the chorus pedal. Lost in its repetitiveness.

fuzzywolf: A very good cover, but they didn't take it further than a cover.

hclapp219 This one is a winner in my mind. It's a cover of the song, but you've put your own spin on it. And I think you took more risks with it -- lyrics, interpretation of the original song, etc.

Judge bronzetalon

GEARMOND: Ah! A decisively abstract cover! Very No-Wave or New Noise, or No-thing. I'm fond of re-imagining. Very well plated. I hear (taste?) notes of super fuzz! Very enticing to my palate, although it's a bit of an acquired taste. It also appears the tone is near-seasick, ESPECIALLY at the end. Incredibly intense.

BLAHQUAKER: OOOOOOOH. I'm immediately bombarded by an overwhelming flavor of oscillator. INCREDIBLY BUZZY. The vocal (vocal?) track are rather vibrato'd, I'm enjoying it. It's almost as if a man that is a bastard created this dish for my ear's displeasure.

SKULLSERVANT: From the onset, I am blasted by overwhelming low end! Tasty! The bass shook my roommate(OH, I mean, co-judge)'s PS2 controller out of the system! Oh my! As the guitars come in, I am increasingly frightened by the slowly creeping in pitch vibrato. This is the sound of my fears! What flavor! Wonderful use of dynamics.

ZROBERTEZ: Chorus ahoy! So many different tones of chorus. A heavy 80's influence I taste here. And, the first track thus far with drums! Hey hey! All in all, a delightful, light and airy interpretation. Very well executed.

BRONZETALON: UPRIGHT BAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAASS! Such incredibly ballsy upright bass. Like, incredibly ballsy. I can taste the balls in my mouth! (Chankgeez, shush) AND NOISE! I sense the use of a cosmichorus! Very fine choice. And vocals! Creeeeeeeeeeepy vocals. Such an eerie overall package.

FUZZYWOLF&HELTER: Ooh! Garnish. I like garnish. Plus, the quiet intro. Very cool. Almost like Bongzilla. The drums sound great, though are like, comically simplified. I like it though. AND THEN THE CHORUS KICKS IN OH MAN YEAAAAAH. Real gnarly.

HCLAPP219: WOAH, the vocals have such a beautiful tone! And the clean guitar is very interesting, a bright tone without lacking character. The wiiiiiiiide sounding chorus is very excellent. The contact mic hiss adds a texture that is very interesting to the ear. A very pleasing cover over all.
